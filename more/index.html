I have simply too many hobbies to perform any of them very admirably, but I'm pleased to say that that fact's never much daunted or bothered me. I principally occupy myself with music—listening, attending, and performing—and rock climbing—primarily bouldering. My other pursuits include reading, biking, and trying new restaurants.

*

Music

Music has been a part of my life since I was seven, when I began to learn the piano. The trajectory of music as a hobby was launched almost singularly by the New York State Music Festival, where over two successive summers I saw students my age perform in a way I only imagined professionals could. Its dispensation scaffolded my interest in music long enough for my to come to truly love it.

I have given solo piano recitals in 2016, 2018, 2020, and 2022, as well as nearly one hundred performances of solo and chamber music with others. My favorite composers to play are Bach, Beethoven, and Schubert.

Climbing

There is no hobby I've fallen for so immediately and thoroughly as climbing. As a naturally unathletic child, scraping along in high school thanks to my occasional biking, starting to climb felt for me much like the first time I ever wore glasses: a moment of realization, "So that's what the big fuss is about."

I began climbing in the spring of 2018. Within six months, I had sent V6; in just over a year, V8. The pandemic forced me outdoors, where I pushed my grade on rock from V3 to V8 in the span of a spring and summer. Today, I enthusiastically climb on rock and plastic; I am currently working on Chossuary, V9/10.
